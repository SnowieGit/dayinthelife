A Day in the Life

Celebrating Sisters

by Bonnie J. Scott


Bonnie Scott


Bonnie Scott is a soon-to-be empty nester trying to squeeze every bit of fun out of life with her two daughters before they go away to college in two weeks. Sunday, August 5 was National Sister's Day 2018. It was also a day of life's simple pleasures.



Start


"All siblings have the odd argument here and there, but deep down there's little way around the natural connection between siblings.




6:10 a.m.

Wake up to the sound of barking and giggling. A 45lb samoyed and 15 pekingese are fighting over the spot on the floor next to Patra, my soon-to-be college freshman. As I walk down the hall, I yell to my oldest, Patrice to take Snowie, the sammi outside. Patra and Lola continue exercising. I peek in the living room.

"It's National Sister's Day," Patra exhales between sit-ups." "Great! That gives you and your sister a good excuse to give Snowie and Lola a good brushing this morning. You and your sister can take them for a long walk after breakfast. They're sisters too afterall. Let them enjoy their day."


6:15 a.m.-7:00a.m.

The three of us prepare and eat breakfast. We also laugh and share our various social media feeds.


7:00-8:00

Clean kitchen, and as many other cleaning tasks as can fit into an hour.


8:00-8:45 a.m.

Call my own sister, Lynn, tell her it's National Sisters Day and arrange to meet up for an 11:00 early movie.Then shower and get dressed so I don't have to compete for bathroom space when girls have finished brushing the dogs.

Patra works doesn't get off till 6:00. She gives us a tour then fixes us some high protein smoothies. Patrice around Patra clown around at the front desk while I sneak in a workout and walk on the ellipticals since I have a member- ship.

We all check our McDonald's Apps and discover Free Fries today! (Free large fries at that). So we walk around the corner to McDonalds and place our orders. We shoot the breeze and I take a picture of the two sisters, my two daughters to commemmorate the day.
